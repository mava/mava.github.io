Description

This course is designed to provide a supervised independent learning experience to approximate a research experience. The student researches 2–3 topics in the mathematical literature and reports their findings in the form of an integrative presentation in front of peers and faculty, mimicking the work of a mathematician in an academic or industrial setting. Successful completion of this course is enough to satisfy the capstone experience requirement for the Master’s (MA) Degree in Mathematics. (See the University Graduate Bulletin).

Grading

Presentations (50%) and class participation (50%).
